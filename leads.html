Project Leads

Alex Doknjas

Alexander Doknjas is the Technical Lead for UVSD. Alexander is a 4th year Electrical Engineering student at the university of Victoria. Alexander has been on the UVSD team for two years and worked on ECOSat-II and ECOSat-III before taking over as technical lead for the Homathko satellite. Alexander is interested in highly integrated DC-DC power systems, energy harvesting and high-reliable applications engineering with a focusing on aerospace and defense applications. Personal interests and hobbies include being an engineering student with no free time, riding mountain bikes, photography, playing Texas hold’em, and spending lots of time realizing building satellites is hard.


Sam Wheating

Sam is a 3rd Year Computer Engineering Student. Having been involved with the club for several years he now works on the Onboard Computer (OBC) team and contributes to the development of test and flight software. He also participates in outreach activities and represents the team at community events. In the future he pursue a career in either aerospace technology or machine learning and high power computing. In his free time, Sam enjoys outdoor sports such as hiking and cycling and participates in competitive programming events whenever possible.


Ben Mazerolle

Ben Mazerolle is a 2nd year Software Engineering student and the Onboard Computer (OBC) software lead. He currently works as a Quality Assurance Co-op student a Reliable Controls, and has been a member of the UVSD since September 2017. He is also involved in the UVSD’s marketing and outreach team and participates in most outreach events and the team’s media projects. Ben has a passion for machine learning and aerospace technology, with a particular interest in the F-35 Lightning II project, and hopes to have a career in autonomous flight software. In his free time, Ben enjoys skiing, mountain biking, and competitive powerlifting.


Ryan Russell

Ryan is a second year Software Engineering student with a devotion to quality and a passion for technology. He currently works as a Quality Assurance co-op student at Latitude Geographics, where he works with software for Geographic Information Systems. As part of UVSD, he works on developing software for the Satellite’s Onboard Computer and helping to guide the team’s project tracking. Throughout his two years at UVIC, Ryan has also competed in a variety of competitions, including Senior Design at the Western Engineering Competition and the Battlesnake Programming Competition. Looking to the future, Ryan hopes to continue working with UVSD to learn both technical and leadership skills that translate to the rapidly expanding software development and aerospace engineering industries.
